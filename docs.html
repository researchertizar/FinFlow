Overview

FinFlow is a personal finance OS that runs entirely in your browser. There is no server, no account, no sync. All data is stored in your browser's localStorage — it never leaves your device.

Fully Private

No login. No cloud. No tracking. Your financial data stays on your device only.

Offline First

Install as a PWA and use it without internet. Currency rates are cached for 6 hours.

Instant

No loading screens. Record a transaction in under 5 seconds.

Three Modes

Simple, Standard, or Pro — the UI adapts to your experience level.

Go to Settings → App & Install → Load Sample Data to explore FinFlow with realistic demo data before entering your own.

Onboarding

On your first visit, FinFlow shows a welcome screen to set up your profile in 30 seconds.

1

Enter your name

Optional — used for the dashboard greeting ("Good morning, Alex!"). Stored locally only.

2

Choose your currency

Defaults to ₹ (Indian Rupee). 45+ currencies available. Change anytime in Settings.

3

Pick experience level

Simple, Standard or Pro — controls which features and labels are shown throughout the app.

If you dismiss onboarding, you can re-open it by clearing site data in your browser, or change all settings under Settings → Profile.

Dashboard

The dashboard is your financial snapshot for the current month. Use the month navigation arrows to browse history.

Profile Header

If you've set your name, a greeting appears at the top ("Good morning, Tizar!") with your avatar. Tap the avatar to go to Settings. The greeting updates automatically based on time of day.

Balance Hero

The large number shows your Total Net Worth — sum of all account balances. The arrow indicator shows change vs the previous month.

Stat Cards

Four cards show this month's Income, Expenses, Net Savings, and Transaction count — each with a vs-last-month comparison.

Charts

Last 6 Months — area chart showing income vs expense trends.

Recent Activity

Last 5 transactions. Tap "See All" to go to the full Transactions view.

Budget Status + Goals

Quick overview of your budgets and savings goals. Tap "Manage" or "All Goals" to drill in.

Quick Action Buttons

Spent Money, Got Paid, Move Money, and Reports — one tap to the most common actions.

The Welcome Card appears on the dashboard when you have no accounts or transactions yet. Tap the 3 steps to get started quickly.

Transactions

Every financial event is a transaction. There are three types:

TypeWhen to useExample
ExpenseMoney going outGroceries, rent, fuel, subscriptions
IncomeMoney coming inSalary, freelance, gifts, refunds
TransferMoving between your own accountsSavings deposit, credit card payment

Adding a Transaction

1

Tap + Add or the circle button

Available from topbar, dashboard quick buttons, or bottom navigation.

2

Choose the type

Spent Money / Got Paid / Move Money (or Expense / Income / Transfer in Pro mode).

3

Enter amount

Type the amount or use the quick-chips (10, 50, 100, 500, 1000, 5000).

4

Fill in details

Description, date, category, and account. All required except description.

Filters

Filter by type, category, account, or keyword. Sort newest/oldest/largest/smallest. Filters apply instantly.

Export

Tap CSV to download all transactions as a spreadsheet. Use Settings → Export Backup for full JSON backup.

Accounts

An account is any place you keep money. Add one account per real-world account you want to track.

🏦 Bank AccountEveryday current/checking account

💳 Credit CardEnter current balance as negative (e.g. -8000 = owed ₹8,000)
📈 InvestmentStocks, mutual funds, crypto
💵 Cash / WalletPhysical cash in hand or digital wallets (UPI, PayTM)
🏠 LoanHome loan, car loan, personal loan — enter as negative

Account Detail

Tap any account card to see a running balance ledger — all transactions for that account with a running total after each entry.

For credit cards, enter the starting balance as a negative number. As you record expense transactions, FinFlow tracks what you owe. Record a Transfer from your bank when you pay the bill.

Budgets

A budget is a monthly spending limit for a category. FinFlow shows you a progress bar and alerts you when you're getting close.

Budget Status Colors

ColorMeaning
GreenOn track — spending is within budget
Amber / StripedWarning — you've used 80%+ of budget
RedOver budget — spending exceeds the limit

Rollover (Standard/Pro mode)

Enable "Roll over unused budget" to carry forward unspent money to next month's limit. E.g. if you budget ₹5,000 for dining and spend ₹3,000, next month's budget becomes ₹7,000.

Budget progress is calculated for the currently selected month — use the month navigator arrows in the topbar to review past months.

Savings Goals

Goals track progress toward a financial target — a vacation fund, emergency reserve, new device, or anything you're saving for.

Creating a Goal

1

Name your goal

"Emergency Fund", "Trip to Goa", "New Laptop" — be specific.

2

Set the target amount

How much total you need to reach this goal.

3

Enter saved so far

If you've already saved some amount toward this goal, enter it here.

FinFlow sends an alert when you reach 75% and again at 100% of your goal. Goals are visible on the dashboard and in the Goals view.

Goals are Pro mode only by default. Enable them by switching to Pro mode in Settings → Profile → Experience Mode.

The right panel shows the total monthly cost of all your recurring items (adjusted for frequency — a yearly payment is divided by 12).

Alerts

FinFlow alerts you when a recurring item is overdue (past due date) or due within 3 days. Check the bell icon in the topbar.

Categories

Categories are labels for your transactions. They power the budget system, reports, and insights.

FinFlow seeds 11 default categories on first launch: Groceries, Dining Out, Transport, Utilities, Entertainment, Health, Shopping, Rent, Salary, Freelance, and Transfer.

Creating a Custom Category

Tap Add in the Categories view. Choose a name, type (expense or income), color, and icon. Once created, it appears in all transaction and budget dropdowns.

Deleting a category will not delete transactions in that category — they'll show as "Uncategorized". Reassign them first if needed.

Currency Rate Source

Rates are fetched from @fawazahmed0/currency-api (free, no API key, hosted on jsDelivr CDN). Rates update every ~24 hours at the source. FinFlow caches them for 6 hours in localStorage. When offline, the last cached rates are used with an amber "Offline — approximate rates" indicator.

FAQ

Will I lose my data if I clear the browser?

Yes. FinFlow stores data in localStorage, which is cleared when you clear site data. Always export a backup first via Settings → Export Backup.

Can I use FinFlow on multiple devices?

Data doesn't sync between devices — it's local-only by design. You can export a JSON backup on one device and import it on another.

Why is the currency rate wrong?

Rates are fetched from a free open API and cached for 6 hours. They're close approximations, not guaranteed live rates. For precise conversions, use a bank or financial service.

What's the storage limit?

Browsers typically allow 5–10MB of localStorage per origin. FinFlow displays current usage in Settings. At 5,000 transactions with all fields, usage is approximately 2–3MB.

How do I reset and start fresh?

Go to Settings → Erase All Data. This permanently deletes everything. Alternatively, clear site data in your browser's privacy settings.

Is there a web or desktop version?

FinFlow is a web app that works in any modern browser. Install it as a PWA for a near-native experience on both mobile and desktop.

Can I add my own categories?

Yes — go to Categories → Add. Custom categories appear in all transaction and budget dropdowns immediately.
